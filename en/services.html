Our Services

Using state-of-the-art equipment and examination methods, eye diseases can often be diagnosed in their early stages before symptoms appear, allowing treatment to begin promptly.

Preventive Care and Basic Diagnostics

  • Visual acuity testing (refraction) incl. prescription
  • Intraocular pressure measurement (tonometry)
  • Color vision test (Ishihara)
  • Central 10° visual field assessment (Amsler grid test)
  • Stereoscopic vision assessment (Lang and Titmus tests)
  • Examination of the anterior segment
  • Fundus examination (ophthalmoscopy)
  • Optic nerve assessment (disc & nerve fiber layer)
  • Glaucoma screening (green star)
  • Cataract preliminary assessment (grey star)
  • Early detection & management of age-related macular degeneration (AMD)
  • Evaluation in hypertension, diabetes and vascular occlusions
  • Interdisciplinary care for thyroid eye disease (endocrine orbitopathy)
  • Regular follow-up in rheumatologic disease (uveitis)
  • Neuro-ophthalmology (e.g. pupil disorders, diplopia, optic nerve pathologies)
  • Evaluation of eye pain, redness, itching
  • Management of dry eye disease
  • Treatment of lid and lid margin inflammation
  • Removal of foreign bodies

Advanced Diagnostic Imaging

  • Autorefraction
  • Tonometry (non-contact & Goldmann applanation)
  • Optical coherence tomography (OCT)
  • Laser scanning polarimetry (GDx)
  • Pachymetry (corneal thickness measurement)
  • Gonioscopy
  • Perimetry (visual field testing)
  • Nyctometry (mesopic vision & glare sensitivity)
  • Tear film analysis (e.g. dry eye)
  • Pupil reaction & diameter, contrast and color vision testing
  • Exophthalmometry (Hertel)

Laser Treatment

  • Diagnosis of posterior capsule opacification after cataract surgery
  • Counselling on YAG capsulotomy (procedure, benefits, risks)
  • Preparation (e.g. pupil dilation if needed)
  • YAG capsulotomy: gentle opening of opacified posterior capsule to improve vision
  • Immediate post-procedure check incl. intraocular pressure
  • Short-term follow-up and anti-inflammatory prophylaxis if required

Driver's License Reports

  • Distance and near visual acuity testing
  • Visual field screening
  • Color vision test (Ishihara)
  • Stereopsis test
  • Nyctometry / glare sensitivity if required
  • Ophthalmic assessment according to German licensing regulations (FeV)
  • Issuance of official certificate

Emergencies

  • Acute vision loss or sudden visual field defect
  • Severe ocular or orbital pain
  • Sudden increase in flashes, showers of floaters or many new floaters
  • Acute redness with photophobia or excessive tearing
  • Suspected acute glaucoma attack (painful hard eye, halos)
  • Foreign body in the eye (metal, dust, wood etc.)
  • Chemical burns (immediate rinsing then urgent assessment)
  • Blunt trauma to eye or orbit
  • Suspected perforation / penetrating injury (urgent evaluation)
  • Acute inflammation of cornea, uvea (iritis) or lid margin
